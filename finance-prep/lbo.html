1. Sources & Uses: The Fundamental Equation

Before any model works, you must balance the transaction. This is the "Setup" phase.

Uses (Where the money goes):
+ Equity Purchase Price (Buying the shares)
+ Refinancing Existing Debt (Paying off old loans)
+ Transaction Fees (Bankers, Lawyers)
= Total Uses

Sources (Where the money comes from):
+ New Debt (Loans, Bonds)
+ New Equity (Check from the PE Firm)
= Total Sources (Must equal Uses)

Critical Insight: The size of the "New Equity" check is the "Plug". We calculate uses, raise max debt, and the difference is what we must pay.

2. The Capital Stack Hierarchy

Not all debt is created equal. Think of it like a sinking ship; who gets into the lifeboats first?

3. The Cash Sweep Waterfall Algorithm

This is the engine of the LBO. How do we pay down debt using free cash flow?

Engineering Perspective: The Algorithm

Every year, the company generates Cash Flow Available for Debt Repayment (CFADS). The logic follows a strict if/else sequence:

1. Mandatory Amortization: Pay required TLA principal.
2. Cash Sweep: Calculate (CFADS - Mandatory Amort).
3. Sweep Logic:
   IF (Revolver Balance > 0): Pay Revolver.
   ELSE IF (Term Loan A > 0): Pay Term Loan A.
   ELSE IF (Term Loan B > 0): Pay Term Loan B.
   ELSE: Accumulate Cash on Balance Sheet.

4. Returns Attribution Analysis

You made a 25% IRR. Great. Why? Did you create value, or did you just get lucky with the market?

Decomposing the MOIC

We break down the returns into three distinct drivers:

  1. EBITDA Growth: Operational improvement. (The best kind of return).
  2. Multiple Expansion: Selling at a higher P/E than you bought. (Luck/Market sentiment).
  3. Deleveraging: Paying down debt using cash flow. (The mechanics of LBO).
Total_Return ($) = Exit_Equity - Entry_Equity

Return_Growth = (EBITDA_Exit - EBITDA_Entry) * Entry_Multiple
Return_Multiple = EBITDA_Exit * (Exit_Multiple - Entry_Multiple)
Return_DebtPaydown = Total_Return - Return_Growth - Return_Multiple

5. The Math of MOIC & IRR

A "Paper LBO" requires instant mental math. Memorize these geometric relationships.

MOIC Time Horizon Implied IRR
2.0x 5 Years ~15%
2.5x 5 Years ~20%
3.0x 5 Years ~25%
2.0x 3 Years ~25%

Rule of 72 / 115 / 144:
To double your money (2x) in 5 years: 72 / 5 = 14.4% IRR.
To triple your money (3x) in 5 years: 115 / 5 = 23% IRR.
