1. Purchase Price Allocation (PPA) Mechanics

When Company A buys Company B for $100M, but Company B's Book Value is only $40M, the remaining $60M is not just "Goodwill". We must allocate this excess purchase price to specific assets first.

The Write-Up Process

We "Write Up" (Increase the value of) tangible and intangible assets to their Fair Market Value (FMV). This creates a new accounting reality:

  • Identifiable Intangibles: Customer lists, Patents, Trademarks. These have finite lives and must be amortized.
  • Deferred Tax Liability (DTL): If we write up an asset on GAAP books but not Tax books, we create a DTL.
    DTL = (Write_Up_Amount) * Tax_Rate
  • Goodwill: The "Plug" variable. Whatever remains after allocating to Intangibles and DTLs.

2. Tax Structuring: Stock vs Asset Deals

The structure of the deal defines the cash flow available for debt service. This is often more important than the price itself.

Feature Stock Deal (Common) Asset Deal / 338(h)(10)
What is bought? Legal Entity (and all hidden liabilities). Specific Assets & Liabilities only.
Tax Basis Carryover Basis: No change in asset values for tax purposes. Step-up Basis: Assets re-valued to purchase price for tax.
Depreciation Based on old (low) book value. Low tax shield. Based on new (high) price. Massive Tax Shield.
Seller Preference High (Single layer of tax on capital gains). Low (Double taxation: Corporate + Individual).
Optimization Algo

An Asset Deal creates value (Tax Shield) but costs the Seller more (Double Tax).
The Solution: The Buyer pays the Seller slightly more to cover their extra tax bill, and they split the remaining Tax Shield value (Pareto Improvement).

3. Advanced Accretion/Dilution Math

Forget the shortcuts. To calculate the exact impact, you must solve for the Breakeven Synergies.

Breakeven Pre-Tax Synergies Formula:

Synergies = [ (Acq_PE / Target_PE) * (1 + Premium) - 1 ] * Target_NI
-------------------------------------------------------------
(1 - Tax_Rate)

This formula tells you: "How much cost cutting ($) do I need just to stop my EPS from falling?" If the required number is realistic, the deal proceeds. If it requires cutting 50% of staff, the deal is dead.

4. Contribution Analysis

In a Merger of Equals (MOE), how do we decide ownership split? We look at what each party "Contributes".

The Ownership Matrix We calculate ownership % implied by various metrics:
  • Based on Revenue: Co_A contributes 60%
  • Based on EBITDA: Co_A contributes 75% (More profitable)
  • Based on Net Income: Co_A contributes 50% (High leverage)

Conclusion: If Co_A gets only 50% ownership (based on NI), they are being diluted on an EBITDA basis. They will demand a higher ownership stake.

5. Game Theory in Bidding (The Winner's Curse)

M&A is an auction. The winner is often the one who overestimates the value the most.

Engineering Perspective: Nash Equilibrium

In a blind auction, your optimal bid $B$ is a function of your valuation $V$ and the number of bidders $n$.

The Winner's Curse states that if you win, you likely paid more than the consensus value. To avoid this, rational bidders shade their bids downwards. In M&A modeling, we must perform "Accretion at various Bid Prices" to find the "Walk-Away Price" (The Reservation Price).
